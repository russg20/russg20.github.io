Description of Target Audience:

The Weather-R-Us website was created to target those individuals in the Preston, Soda Springs, and Fish Haven areas of Idaho who need a full and immediate forecast of the weather in those areas. It is meant to be used by male and female individuals who are between the ages of 9 to 100 and who are familiar with weather conditions and phrases.


Personas:

Jerry

Jerry is a 52-year old dairy farmer who is married with four children. He holds a bachelor's degree in dairy science and owns a medium-sized dairy with approximately 500 cows. His salary is around $45,000 per year. Frequently throughout the day Jerry checks the weather on his phone or computer so that he can plan when to harvest his crops, when to open or close up the barn, when the field conditions will allow for farm equipment access, and when to artificially inseminate his cows for appropriate birth months for the anticipated calves.

Kathy

Kathy is a 35-year old domestic engineer (AKA stay-at-home mom) who is married with two children, one of which has severe allergies. Kathy does not earn an income, however, her husband is a Mechanical Engineer and brings in an annual income of $52,000. Kathy has a bachelor's degree in nursing, but has not worked in that field since her children were born. She references weather conditions on her phone throughout the day to know when it is safe for her son to go outdoors and to regulate his medications. Checking the weather also allows her to ensure that her children are appropriately dressed for current conditions.

Scenarios:

  1. Will the air quality be adequate for me to go running today?
  2. Should I wear a raincoat to work tomorrow?
  3. Should I bundle up my kids to go to school today?
  4. Do I need to make sure I bring sunglasses on my walk this morning?
  5. Will the fields be dry enough to allow the use of farm equipment?
  6. Will the pollen count be low enough for me to forego taking my allergy meds?
  7. Do I need to cover my garden plants with plastic tonight?
  8. Can I cut my hay tomorrow?
