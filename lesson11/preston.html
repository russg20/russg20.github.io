Winter in the Air

by Ginger Russ

If you haven’t noticed already, winter appears to have fallen on us quickly. It seems like only yesterday that children were playing in the park and parents were wrestling with the mountains of autumn leaves in their yards. Now we have snow in the forecast and daily temperatures hover below freezing. Although winter conditions are not unusual for our neck of the woods, every year it takes some adjusting as snow and freezing temperatures become the norm. Ethel Cowley, a long-time resident of Preston, commented on the approaching snow, saying that she never gets used to the cold, wet winters in Preston. Ethel’s least favorite part of the winter season is shoveling snow from her walkways and driveway. She appreciates the help of her neighbors who offer to do the shoveling for her on those bitter cold mornings when she needs go out. I’m sure all of us can empathize with Ethel in this matter. How grateful we are for helpful neighbors!
