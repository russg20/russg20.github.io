A Winter Evening

by Ginger Russ

Have you ever walked the park in the early evening after a snowstorm? There are myriads of sights and sounds to enjoy. I love to hear the crunch of the fresh snow under my boots as I traverse the ground on my way through the park. If you listen carefully you might hear the hoot of an owl as they look for their prey. The snow-enveloped trees are beautiful to see, shining bright in the moonlight. They make me think of a gingerbread house with its ice-cream cone trees covered in icing. The darkness envelops me as I continue my walk. The sky is clear and dark with shimmering stars dotting the expanse. There is a special feeling in the air as I consider all of the creations of God and how minute and inconsequential I am in all of them – seemingly inconsequential and yet so loved by Him. He created all these things for me and you for our enjoyment and consideration. I am amazed at the depth of His love and wisdom. As my walk concludes I am filled with gratitude for the world in which we live and the many beauties of nature. Won’t you join me next time for a walk in the park?
